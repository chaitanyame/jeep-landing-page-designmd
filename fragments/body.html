SINCE 1941

EIGHTY YEARS OF TRAIL-RATED HERITAGE

Born from the demands of the Second World War, the original Jeep was engineered in just forty-nine days to carry soldiers across battlefields no road map could describe. Its go-anywhere chassis became a symbol of resilience, and when the guns fell quiet, returning veterans carried that legend home, pressing those same rugged machines into farmwork, forestry, and the long dirt roads of a changing America.

From that utilitarian beginning emerged the Trail Rated badge — earned only after a vehicle proves itself across traction, ground clearance, maneuverability, articulation, and water fording. Eight decades later, every Jeep that wears the seven-slot grille still answers to that same standard: to go where the pavement ends and keep going, without apology or concession.

JEEP REVEALS THE 2025 WRANGLER RUBICON 392

The most powerful production Wrangler yet returns with a 6.4L V8 delivering 470 horsepower to all four wheels. Production remains limited, with allocations opening to reservation holders this winter.

GLADIATOR EARNS TRAIL RATED BOILD NOD

The midsize pickup completes its Trail Rated certification across five off-road capability categories, extending Jeep's go-anywhere philosophy to the truck bed. Order books open with the 2026 model year.
